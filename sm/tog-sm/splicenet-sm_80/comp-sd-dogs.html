SpliceNet Comparisons: SD-Dogs

We present SpliceNet results of semantic appearance transfer with respect to the recent baselines: (1) Kim. et al [1] (2) Swapping Autoencoders (SA) [2] (3) WCT2 [3] (4) Splice. SpliceNet manages to transfer complex texture on corresponding semantic parts across images with challenging alignments. Note that in some cases, Splice is prone to instabilities during its optimization process, which may lead to incorrect semantic association and poor visual quality. On the other hand, since SpliceNet is trained on a dataset of semantically related image pairs, it results in a better semantic association, higher visual quality, and is more robust to challenging, unaligned input pairs.
